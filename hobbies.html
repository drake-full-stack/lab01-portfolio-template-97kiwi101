  • Haikus

    I at first really dislikes haikus. they felt to resrictive for and art that is supposuded to be freeing. However, after watching Words Bubble Up Like Soda Pop, I realized what they could achieve. This film rekinddled my love for haikues and inpsired me to create haikus. Something I now pride myself on now. here are just some few I am proud of!

    The world turns around

    like the leaves in the air breeze

    Both waiting to bend

    A rock sinks downwards

    unable to float once more

    slowly forgotten

    In the darkest night

    every star had been placed

    bounded by a line

    A boulder rest on a hill

    sadden by its state

    Unable to move or go

    High above a bridge

    a leaf holds on scared to fall

    wanting to fly high

    The reason I continue to write haikues is its an easy form to figure out how you are feeling. You must be exact in your wording and can not form complex fluffy sentences that hints at what you are trying to say. this direact approch makes it harder, but also more grounding to one self
